>
> **Canonical lines — keep verbatim:** *"Anyone can say it. A card makes you pay for it."* · *"You play the actor that produces the outcome, not the price of it."* · *"Practise the future. ## The spine (five lines, in order)

Frame → differentiation → mechanic → moat → promise, in one breath:

1. **Meridians is a cognitive operating system for teams.**
2. **Most tools store what your team wrote down. Meridians runs what your team believes.**
3. **Anyone can say it. A card makes you pay for it.**
4. **The fiftieth session is nothing like the tenth.**
5. **Practise the future. Earn the morning the surprise lands.**

## Headlines

- Meridians is a cognitive operating system for teams.
- The operating system for what hasn't happened yet.
- Your team already runs on something. Make it something that compounds.
- Your judgement, finally running on something.

## Runs — judgement, not files

- Most tools store what your team wrote down. Meridians runs what your team believes.
- It doesn't index your documents. It runs your judgement.
- Your files are what you wrote down. Your judgement is what you'd do. We run the second.
- A deck is read once and dies. An OS runs.

## State — persistent, shared, arbitrated

- The meeting ends, the thinking evaporates. Meridians keeps it.
- A shared ledger of where your team stands — queryable, not remembered.
- The room forgets. The substrate keeps the ledger.
- One source of truth for what your team believes and how it decides.

## Compounds — the moat

- The fiftieth session is nothing like the tenth. That gap is the product.
- Judgement that compounds for the team that built it — and no one else.
- A foundation model ships "Strategy Mode" in a quarter. It can't ship your room's history.
- The one thing that compounds is the one thing no vendor can ship cold.

## The mechanic — commitment over talk

- Anyone can say it. A card makes you pay for it.
- A card is conviction you've already paid for.
- You don't argue your position. You pay for it.
- Words are free. A card is on the record.
- Don't predict the outcome — play the actor who produces it.
- A market gives you a number. A room gives you a role.
- What you said is free. What you played is on the record. The gap is where trust dies.

## The human edge — the vision thesis

- Human vision is humanity's edge over AI.
- Models predict the future. They don't choose which one to make.
- Human-up, not data-down: we surface the judgement that never reached a document.
- You bring the vision. The OS holds the ledger.

## Two modes — Capture & Playback

The vision thesis, exercised two ways — two sides of one coin:

- Playback sells the room. Capture keeps it.
- Capture is what reality *is*. Playback is how it *could go*.
- Capture records the vision. Playback runs it.

## The day-one hook — the coordination drill

- Bring your team. An AI adversary takes the other side and hunts the gaps between you.
- Anyone coordinates in a calm room. Can you hold when something's hunting your seams?
- Find your seams in here, where they're cheap — not out there, where they cost you.
- Every untested team has seams. This is where you find yours, first.
- Session one finds the seam. Session fifty is why you can't leave.

## The Game Master — the unit

- We don't sell a tool to a company. We find Game Masters and arm them.
- Forged in low-stakes play; deployed where the stakes are real.
- The room belongs to its Game Master.
- A Game Master keeps two things: the model, and the people who keep it true.
- Players become Game Masters. The network grows one good table at a time.

## The proof / the engine

- The same math that reads the shape of *Harry Potter* reads the shape of your firm.
- Your organisation already wrote its narrative — in the decisions it actually made.
- Low-temperature in, deterministic out. Same input, same score.
- (Honest footnote, when the room is technical: reproducibility is not validity — the proof is post-hoc on a text we didn't write; forward, adversarial reality is the part still to prove.)

## The promise — closers

- Practise the future. Earn the morning the surprise lands.
- The future arrives once. Run it a hundred times first.
- Most rooms meet the future cold. Yours will have played it.

## Usage notes

- **One frame per surface.** Lead with the OS frame *or* the play-the-actor frame on a given page — don't stack both in the same headline. The spine above interleaves them deliberately, in sequence, not at once.
- **The mechanic carries the deck.** *"Anyone can say it. A card makes you pay for it."* is the most distinctive line in the set — short, concrete, teaches binding commitment in a breath (and "pay" ties to the conviction economy). Earn the abstraction with it before naming machinery (World View, SWF, streams, merges).
- **Don't over-claim the transfer.** The Harry-Potter line is the strongest proof; pair it with the honest footnote whenever the audience can check the math, or it reads as hype to the reader most worth convincing.
- **Short beats chained.** Let the strong lines land as declaratives. The em-dash is a seasoning, not a structure.
