# COPY — Meridians key lines

> The copy bank: the lines that capture Meridians, clustered for reuse across decks, landing pages, and the [manifesto](src/app/manifesto/page.tsx). The frame is two halves of one product: **a gamified rehearsal engine that compounds into a social network.** The **product** is the rehearsal engine — you *play the future before it arrives* (any text becomes a playable world; you rehearse it as a card game on a measured substrate, **human-up**, kept **local**). The **moat and growth** are the **social network it compounds into** — each session's judgement, worlds, and players accrue to something no competitor can ship cold; it grows **grassroots / B2C** through communities around Game Masters. *The engine is what you use; the network is what it becomes.* The mechanic is *commitment over talk* (a card makes you pay for what you believe); the pressure you practise against is **AI**, the board that never stops moving. Under it all, an older claim — **judgement is the one thing an organisation runs on that never had a ledger** — and a humble principle: we use AI to **sharpen human judgement, not replace it.**

## Usage notes

- **Lead with the social-network identity.** The company *is* a social network built on playable models of reality; rehearsal is the activity on it, not the headline. Open with the network/play frame, then earn the machinery. Don't stack two frames (network *and* OS *and* play-the-actor) in one headline — the spine above sequences them deliberately.
- **AI is the pressure, never the pitch.** Use the "moving board / play against the machine" frame to dramatise *why you practise* — but the hero is the human network, not the adversary. Don't let the copy read as an anti-AI stance; the register is humble-at-the-frontier (*"to better our very lives"*), AI as tailwind and sparring partner, not enemy.
- **The mechanic carries the deck.** *"Anyone can say it. A card makes you pay for it."* is the most distinctive line in the set — short, concrete, teaches binding commitment in a breath (and "pay" ties to the conviction economy). Earn the abstraction with it before naming machinery (World View, SWF, streams, merges).
- **The ledger is the close, not the open.** *"Judgement is the one thing an organisation runs on that never had a ledger"* (with the accounting / version-control / lab-notebook ladder) is the deepest line in the set — the claim that outlasts the slogan. Land it after the mechanic has earned the abstraction, when the room is asking what the thing fundamentally *is*. The tailwind line (*"Model improvement is our tailwind, not our threat"*) is its companion for the AI-lab objection.
- **Don't over-claim the transfer.** The Harry-Potter line is the strongest proof; pair it with the honest footnote whenever the audience can check the math, or it reads as hype to the reader most worth convincing.
- **Short beats chained.** Let the strong lines land as declaratives. The em-dash is a seasoning, not a structure.
